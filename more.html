Understanding Hurricanes

Everything you need to know about one of nature's most powerful storms

What is a Hurricane?

A hurricane is a large, organized storm system characterized by a center of low pressure, a closed circulation of winds, and strong thunderstorms that produce heavy rains and winds. These storms can be very powerful, causing significant damage when they make landfall.

How Do Hurricanes Form?

Hurricanes form over warm ocean waters near the equator. The process begins when moist air rises from the warm ocean, and as it moves upward, the air cools and condenses, forming clouds. The rotation of the Earth causes the system to spin, and when the system reaches sufficient size, it can turn into a hurricane.

Famous Hurricanes in History

  • Hurricane Katrina (2005): One of the deadliest hurricanes in US history, causing over 1,800 deaths and widespread damage, particularly in New Orleans.
  • Hurricane Irma (2017): A Category 5 hurricane that caused devastation in the Caribbean and southern US.
  • Hurricane Maria (2017): This hurricane devastated Puerto Rico, causing significant damage to the island`s infrastructure and leaving millions without power.

Impact of Hurricanes

Hurricanes cause both immediate and long-term damage, including flooding (ex.Hurricane Katrina), wind destruction, and loss of life. Coastal communities are especially vulnerable due to storm surges, while inland areas may experience severe flooding. The rebuilding process after a hurricane can take years.
