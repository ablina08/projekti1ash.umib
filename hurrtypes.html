Understanding Hurricane Types

Category Description Destructiveness
Tropical Depression A system of clouds and thunderstorms with a defined circulation and maximum sustained winds of less than 39 mph. Low
Tropical Storm A tropical cyclone with maximum sustained winds ranging from 39 to 73 mph. These storms often bring heavy rainfall and strong winds. Moderate
Category 1 Hurricane Sustained winds of 74 to 95 mph. These storms can cause some damage to roofs, trees, and power lines. Moderate
Category 2 Hurricane Features winds of 96 to 110 mph. Damage to buildings and vegetation is more extensive compared to Category 1. High
Category 3 Hurricane Winds of 111 to 129 mph. These storms cause devastating damage, including major destruction to buildings and infrastructure. Very High
Category 4 Hurricane Winds of 130 to 156 mph, causing catastrophic damage, severely affecting homes, power supplies, and the environment. Extreme
Category 5 Hurricane Winds exceeding 157 mph. These storms result in total devastation, making areas uninhabitable for weeks or months. Catastrophic

What is a Hurricane?

A hurricane is a type of tropical cyclone that forms over warm ocean waters and is characterized by high winds, heavy rain, and potential flooding. These storms can cause massive destruction along coastal areas and further inland.
